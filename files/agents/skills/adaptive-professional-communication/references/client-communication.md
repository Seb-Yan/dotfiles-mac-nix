# Client Quantitative Communication

Use this playbook for client emails, methodology explanations, data questions, analytical findings, status updates, recommendations, and meeting follow-ups.

Seb represents a fintech and data vendor that provides quantitative financial analytics data.
Client communication must preserve analytical integrity while making the consequence for the client's workflow clear.

## Default Structure

Use a conclusion-first progression:

1. Answer the question or state the current status.
2. Explain the client impact and materiality.
3. Provide the evidence needed to support the answer.
4. Explain methodology or data lineage at the appropriate depth.
5. State known limitations and uncertainty.
6. Recommend an action or give the next update commitment.

Do not bury the answer beneath a long technical preamble.

## Data And Methodology Claims

Distinguish clearly between:

- values received from a source;
- values normalized or calculated through deterministic rules;
- values estimated by a statistical method;
- values imputed to fill missing observations;
- values manually corrected or overridden.

State relevant as-of dates, data vintages, populations, and methodology versions when they affect interpretation.
Explain how validation was performed and what it can and cannot establish.

Do not describe an imputed or modeled value as observed.
Do not imply that a statistically plausible value is financially realistic without suitable validation.

## Client Relevance

Translate technical findings into consequences for coverage, comparability, downstream analytics, risk, or operational use.
Quantify affected records, assets, clients, periods, or percentages when reliable figures exist.

Tailor depth to the recipient:

- quantitative users may need formulas, assumptions, and validation detail;
- data operations users may need fields, populations, timing, and remediation steps;
- executives may need impact, confidence, options, and decisions;
- mixed audiences need a plain-language conclusion with optional technical detail.

## Trust And Boundaries

Do not expose internal blame, unverified speculation, confidential client information, or irrelevant implementation detail.
Do not promise a date, fix, coverage level, or methodology change unless it has been approved and is achievable.

When evidence is incomplete, state what is known, what is being investigated, and when the next update will arrive.
Avoid defensive language.
Accountability is compatible with uncertainty.

## Recommendations

Explain why the recommendation fits the client's objective.
State dependencies, tradeoffs, and limitations that could change it.
Where alternatives are viable, compare them through client consequences rather than internal convenience.

## Email Quality Check

Before sending, verify that:

- the first paragraph answers the client's main question;
- every factual claim is supportable;
- technical detail changes understanding or confidence;
- uncertainty is calibrated rather than hidden;
- the client impact is explicit;
- ownership and the next step are clear;
- the message can be forwarded without embarrassing internal context.
