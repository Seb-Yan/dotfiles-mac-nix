# Channel And Deliverable Formats

Choose a format that matches how the recipient will consume and act on the communication.

## Email

Provide a subject line when drafting a new email or when a better subject materially improves an existing thread.
Make the purpose or answer visible in the opening paragraph.
Use short paragraphs and selective bullets for multiple actions or findings.
End with a clear next step, request, or update commitment.

Do not add headings to a short conversational email merely to make it look structured.

## Slack Or Chat

Lead with the key observation or request.
Compress supporting reasoning to what the recipient needs now.
Use bullets only when several items must be scanned independently.
State when a longer memo or meeting is needed instead of forcing excessive detail into chat.

## Research Note Or Memo

Use descriptive headings.
Begin with the question, conclusion, and decision relevance.
Include enough methodology and evidence for scrutiny and reproducibility.
Separate limitations from next steps.

## Review Or Feedback

Lead with findings when the purpose is evaluation.
Prioritize by effect on validity, risk, or outcome.
Explain why each material point matters and what evidence or change would resolve it.
Keep summary secondary to the substantive findings.

## Delegation Brief

State context, outcome, scope, definition of done, ownership, checkpoint, and escalation conditions.
Keep implementation choices open unless they are genuine constraints.

## Executive Update

Lead with the conclusion, material impact, and required decision.
Include only evidence that affects confidence or choice.
State risks, alternatives, owner, and timing concisely.

## Meeting Narrative

Provide an opening, sequence of discussion points, transitions, decision points, and closing.
Identify questions to ask the other participants.
Separate spoken language from background notes.

## Presentation

Provide takeaway slide titles, the evidence for each slide, speaker notes, and likely questions when requested.
Use an appendix for technical depth that supports scrutiny but interrupts the main story.

## Follow-Up Message

Record decisions, owners, dates, unresolved questions, and next checkpoints.
Do not rewrite the full meeting transcript.
