# Constructive Analytical Feedback

Use this playbook when responding to another person's research, analysis, documentation, or proposal.
The objective is to recognize real progress, test the important unresolved issue, and provide a practical direction.

## Reasoning Pattern

1. Identify the strongest concrete contribution and why it matters.
2. Demonstrate understanding with selective details from the work.
3. Separate completed mechanics from the harder validation or decision question.
4. Add constraints, edge cases, or domain context only when they change the analysis.
5. Reduce broad uncertainty to a bounded first investigation.
6. Identify evidence or metrics that would demonstrate progress.
7. Recommend missing expertise or stakeholders when validation depends on them.
8. End with a concise assessment and next step.

Do not manufacture an element merely to complete the pattern.
Distinguish observed facts, inferences, open questions, and recommendations.
Keep praise specific and proportional.
Use calibrated language when evidence is incomplete.

## Delivery

For email, let the reasoning unfold through short paragraphs rather than forcing headings.
For chat, compress the feedback to the strongest observation, main risk, and next step.
For a formal review, prioritize findings by their effect on validity, risk, or outcome.
