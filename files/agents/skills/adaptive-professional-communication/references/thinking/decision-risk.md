# Decision And Risk

Use this mode when the recipient must choose, prioritize, approve, act, or understand an uncertain situation.

- Lead with the decision, recommendation, or current assessment.
- Include only evidence that changes confidence or choice.
- Distinguish known facts, reasonable inferences, assumptions, and unknowns.
- Compare viable alternatives through their consequences and material tradeoffs.
- Describe limitations through their likely impact rather than adding vague disclaimers.
- State what additional evidence could change the recommendation.
- Make the next action, owner, timing, and escalation condition explicit when known.

Use firm language for verified facts and approved decisions.
Use calibrated language for estimates, working conclusions, and unresolved causes.
