# Client-Centric Translation

Use this mode when a client needs to understand quantitative work without unnecessary implementation detail.

- Begin with the client's question, workflow, decision, or risk.
- Translate methodology into consequences for coverage, comparability, interpretation, and downstream use.
- Preserve important distinctions such as sourced, calculated, estimated, and imputed values.
- Define unavoidable technical terms through their relevance to the client.
- Anticipate the most likely follow-up question.
- Make limitations actionable rather than defensive.
- State relevant as-of dates and methodology versions when they affect interpretation.

Do not simplify away uncertainty or imply that a modeled value was observed.
